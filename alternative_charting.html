Discover the most powerful alternative charting techniques used by professional traders for trend analysis, breakout detection, noise reduction, and market psychology.

Charting Techniques

Understand how each chart works and where it performs best.

1. Heikin Ashi

Smooth Candlestick Trends

What it is

A modified candlestick chart using averaged values to smooth price action.

How it works
  • Uses averaged OHLC values
  • Reduces market noise
  • Makes trends easier to identify
Best for
  • Trend following
  • Swing trading
  • Trend strength analysis
Advantages
  • Smooth trend visualization
  • Fewer false signals
  • Easy to understand
Disadvantages
  • Lagging nature
  • Actual price not exact
  • Not ideal for precise entries
Visual Style

Looks like regular candlesticks but smoother.

2. Renko

Brick-Based Trend Charts

What it is

A chart made of bricks that ignores time completely.

How it works
  • New brick forms after fixed price movement
  • Time is ignored entirely
  • Example: ₹10 movement = 1 brick
Best for
  • Trend identification
  • Breakout trading
  • Noise reduction
Advantages
  • Very clean charts
  • Excellent trend clarity
  • Filters small fluctuations
Disadvantages
  • Delayed reversals
  • Brick size is critical
  • No time information
Visual Style

Looks like stacked blocks or bricks.

3. Line Break

Breakout Confirmation Lines

What it is

A chart that creates new lines only when price breaks previous highs/lows.

How it works
  • Most popular: 3-Line Break Chart
  • Reversal only if price exceeds previous 3 lines
Best for
  • Trend reversal detection
  • Medium-term trading
Advantages
  • Less market noise
  • Strong reversal visibility
  • Simpler than Renko
Disadvantages
  • Can lag
  • Limited platform support
Visual Style

Vertical blocks and directional lines.

4. Kagi

Supply & Demand Psychology

What it is

A Japanese charting method focused on supply and demand.

How it works
  • Line thickness changes on reversal
  • Thick line = stronger demand
  • Thin line = stronger supply
Best for
  • Trend reversals
  • Support & resistance
Advantages
  • Excellent trend change detection
  • Removes noise
  • Shows market psychology
Disadvantages
  • Harder to learn
  • Less commonly used
Visual Style

Vertical lines with thickness changes.

5. Point & Figure

X’s and O’s Price Action

What it is

A chart using X’s and O’s to represent price movement.

How it works
  • X = rising prices
  • O = falling prices
  • Uses box size & reversal amount
  • Ignores time completely
Best for
  • Long-term trend analysis
  • Breakout trading
  • Support & resistance
Advantages
  • Very little noise
  • Excellent pattern clarity
  • Strong breakout signals
Disadvantages
  • Learning curve
  • Less intuitive initially
Visual Style

Columns of X’s and O’s.

Which One Should You Use?

Use Heikin Ashi if:

  • You prefer candlestick-style charts
  • You are a beginner
  • You trade trends

Use Renko if:

  • You want clean trend signals
  • You dislike market noise
  • You trade breakouts

Use Line Break if:

  • You want reversal confirmation
  • You prefer simple trend logic

Use Kagi if:

  • You focus on market psychology
  • You want strong reversal analysis

Use Point & Figure if:

  • You love breakout trading
  • You want long-term clarity
  • You analyze support/resistance deeply
