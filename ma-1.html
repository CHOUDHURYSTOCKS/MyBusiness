SMA vs EMA vs WMA vs SMMA vs ALMA

A complete guide to understanding the most popular Moving Averages used in technical analysis and algorithmic trading. Learn how each indicator works, its strengths, weaknesses, and best use cases.

Quick Overview

SMA

Simple average of closing prices over a selected period.

EMA

Gives greater weight to recent price action.

WMA

Weighted average where recent prices have larger influence.

SMMA

Extra smooth moving average that minimizes market noise.

ALMA

Modern low-lag moving average using Gaussian distribution.

1. SMA — Simple Moving Average

SMA is the most basic and widely used moving average. It calculates the arithmetic mean of prices over a specific period. Every price contributes equally to the final value.

SMA = (P1 + P2 + P3 + ... + Pn) / n

Advantages

  • Easy to calculate and understand.
  • Excellent for long-term trend identification.
  • Reduces random market noise.

Disadvantages

  • Responds slowly to trend changes.
  • Produces delayed signals.

2. EMA — Exponential Moving Average

EMA gives more weight to recent prices. As a result, it reacts much faster to market movements than SMA. Many traders prefer EMA for short-term trend analysis.

EMA = Price × Multiplier + Previous EMA × (1 − Multiplier)

Advantages

  • Fast reaction to trend changes.
  • Popular for swing and intraday trading.
  • Works well in momentum strategies.

Disadvantages

  • Can generate false signals.
  • More sensitive to market noise.

3. WMA — Weighted Moving Average

Weighted Moving Average assigns different weights to each price. Recent prices receive larger weights than older prices.

WMA = Σ(Price × Weight) / ΣWeights

Advantages

  • More responsive than SMA.
  • Balances speed and smoothness.
  • Useful for trend-following systems.

Disadvantages

  • More complex calculation.
  • Can still react to noise.

4. SMMA — Smoothed Moving Average

SMMA includes historical data continuously, creating a very smooth curve. It is designed to remove short-term fluctuations.

SMMA = (Previous SMMA × (N−1) + Current Price) / N

Advantages

  • Excellent noise reduction.
  • Smooth trend visualization.
  • Good for long-term trend traders.

Disadvantages

  • Very high lag.
  • Slow entry and exit signals.

5. ALMA — Arnaud Legoux Moving Average

ALMA was developed to solve the lag problem found in traditional moving averages. It uses a Gaussian distribution to provide smoothness while remaining highly responsive.

ALMA = Gaussian Weighted Price Average

Advantages

  • Very low lag.
  • Smooth and responsive.
  • Excellent for algorithmic trading.
  • Reduces false signals.

Disadvantages

  • More complex mathematics.
  • Not available on all platforms.
