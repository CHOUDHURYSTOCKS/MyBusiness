Detailed Breakdown

1. Simple Moving Average (SMA)

What it does

  • Takes the average closing price over a fixed period.
  • Every candle has equal weight.

Characteristics

  • Smooth and stable
  • Slowest to react
  • Filters noise well
  • More lagging

Best For

  • Long-term trend analysis
  • Support/resistance zones
  • Swing trading
  • Macro trend direction

Common Settings

  • 20 SMA
  • 50 SMA
  • 100 SMA
  • 200 SMA

Weakness

Late entries and exits during fast market moves.

2. Exponential Moving Average (EMA)

What it does

  • Gives more weight to recent prices.
  • Reacts faster than SMA.

Characteristics

  • Faster response
  • Less lag
  • More sensitive to volatility
  • More false signals in choppy markets

Best For

  • Intraday trading
  • Momentum trading
  • Crypto/forex scalping
  • Trend continuation entries

Common Settings

  • 9 EMA
  • 20 EMA
  • 50 EMA

Why Traders Like It

EMAs catch trend changes earlier than SMAs.

3. Weighted Moving Average (WMA)

What it does

  • Assigns increasing weight to recent prices.
  • More aggressive than EMA in some implementations.

Characteristics

  • Faster than SMA
  • Sharper than EMA
  • Very responsive

Best For

  • Short-term systems
  • Fast trend detection
  • Mean reversion systems

Weakness

Can become noisy during volatile market conditions.

4. Moving Average Channel

What it is

A channel built around a moving average:

  • Upper Band = MA + Offset
  • Lower Band = MA − Offset

Offsets May Use

  • Percentage
  • ATR
  • Standard deviation

Characteristics

  • Shows trend + volatility together
  • Identifies overextension

Typical Use

  • Upper band → bullish momentum
  • Lower band → bearish momentum
  • Midline → trend equilibrium

Similar To

Bollinger Bands & Keltner Channels.

5. Moving Average Hamming

What it is

A moving average using a Hamming window from digital signal processing.

Characteristics

  • Very smooth
  • Reduces noise significantly
  • Less whipsaw
  • More computational

Best For

  • Advanced technical analysis
  • Algorithmic trading
  • Signal filtering
  • Low-noise trend detection

Weakness

  • More lag than EMA/WMA
  • Less common on retail platforms

Important Note

Hamming averages come from DSP (Digital Signal Processing), not classical trading indicators.

6. ALMA (Arnaud Legoux Moving Average)

What it does

  • Uses Gaussian smoothing
  • Uses adjustable offset
  • Uses adjustable sigma

Designed To

  • Reduce lag
  • Keep smoothness

Characteristics

  • Very smooth
  • Faster than SMA
  • Less noisy than EMA
  • Excellent trend clarity

Why Traders Love It

  • Cleaner trends
  • Fewer fake reversals
  • Better visual structure

Best For

  • Trend-following
  • Swing trading
  • Crypto markets
  • Automated systems

Typical Settings

  • Length: 9–21
  • Offset: 0.85
  • Sigma: 6
