Detailed Indicator Breakdown

Understand each indicator deeply with strengths, weaknesses, and best use cases.

1. Ichimoku Cloud

Complete Trading System

Combines trend direction, momentum, support/resistance, and entry signals into one advanced indicator.

  • Best for swing & positional trading
  • Excellent trend filtering
  • Powerful on higher timeframes
  • Can overwhelm beginners initially
2. Bollinger Bands

Volatility & Mean Reversion

Measures volatility around a moving average using upper and lower bands.

  • Excellent for squeeze detection
  • Great in ranging markets
  • Useful for reversals
  • Can produce fake breakouts during trends
3. MACD

Momentum & Trend Confirmation

Tracks trend momentum using moving averages and histogram expansion.

  • Clean trend signals
  • Great for swing trading
  • Easy divergence spotting
  • Lagging in fast markets
4. RSI

Overbought/Oversold Momentum

Measures momentum speed using traditional 70/30 levels.

  • Simple and beginner friendly
  • Excellent with price action
  • Good divergence indicator
  • Can remain extreme in strong trends
5. Stochastic Oscillator

Fast Reversal Signals

Measures price relative to recent trading range.

  • Very fast signals
  • Excellent for scalping
  • Sensitive turning points
  • Highly noisy in trends
6. SuperTrend

Simple Trend Following

ATR-based trend-following indicator designed for easy buy/sell decisions.

  • Very beginner friendly
  • Simple visual trend changes
  • Great for intraday trends
  • Weak in choppy markets
7. Keltner Channels

Smoother Volatility Channel

Uses ATR instead of standard deviation for cleaner trend-following behavior.

  • Cleaner than Bollinger Bands
  • Great for trend continuation
  • Less reactive and smoother
  • May miss early reversals
8. Donchian Channels

Breakout Trading System

Tracks highest highs and lowest lows to identify major breakouts.

  • Famous in Turtle Trading
  • Captures massive trends
  • Excellent breakout strategy
  • Often enters late
